Customer Churn Analysis (Python)

Conducted in-depth Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on telecom customer churn data using Python to identify key drivers influencing customer attrition. Leveraged statistical analysis and visual storytelling to uncover behavioral, demographic, and contract-based patterns impacting churn rates.

Key Insights & Findings:

  • Churn Rate Analysis: Identified overall churn rate of 26.54%, indicating significant customer attrition risk.
  • Demographic Risk Factor: Senior citizens exhibited a noticeably higher churn probability compared to younger customers.
  • Tenure Impact: Customers with shorter tenure (0–10 months) showed the highest churn tendency, highlighting early lifecycle risk.
  • Contract Type Influence: Month-to-month contract customers churned significantly more than 1-year and 2-year contract holders.
  • Payment Behavior Insight: Electronic check users demonstrated the highest churn rates among all payment methods.
  • Service Adoption Effect: Customers without value-added services (Online Security, Backup, Device Protection, Tech Support) were more likely to churn.
  • Visualization & Analysis: Developed 15+ analytical visualizations (count plots, stacked bars, histograms, distribution plots) to support actionable retention strategies.

Tech Stack:

Python | Pandas | NumPy | Data Cleaning | Exploratory Data Analysis | Matplotlib | Seaborn | Jupyter Notebook

Zepto vs Blinkit Dashboard (Power BI)

Key Insights:

  • Total Sales: 664K combined sales analyzed, with peak performance in June (107K) and July (89K).

  • Customer Ratings: Average rating of 2.99/5, highlighting potential service quality improvement areas.
  • Payment Behavior: UPI (35.9%) emerged as the dominant mode, followed by Card (32.81%) and COD (31.29%), indicating strong digital payment adoption.
  • Category Analysis: Performance evaluated across Bakery, Grains, Beverages, Poultry, Dairy, and Snacks.
  • Monthly Sales Trend: Highest growth observed in June–July; lowest in October–November (25K each).
  • Year-over-Year Comparison: Comparative performance analysis between 2024 and 2025 to identify growth trajectory.
